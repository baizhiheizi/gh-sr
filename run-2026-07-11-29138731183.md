## Discovered contracts (for future reference)
- **`strfmt.FmtFloat` is the canonical helper for `strconv.AppendFloat`** — used by `tui.formatPercent`, `tui.formatUsedTotal`, `runner.FormatBytesHuman`, and now `host.LoadStr`. The previous LoadStr stuck with `strconv.FormatFloat` + `strings.Builder` only because it predated the `strfmt` package.
- **`strings.Builder.Grow(N)` allocates** even when `N` is small enough to fit on a stack buffer — observed in the 24 → 16 B/op delta.
- **`var b [N]byte; b := strfmt.FmtFloat(b[:0], v, prec)` then `string(b)`** is the canonical pattern: zero-alloc except for the final `string(b)` cast (one alloc), no Builder overhead, no per-call `FormatFloat` return.
